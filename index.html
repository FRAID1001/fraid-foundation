FRAID Foundation was born out of a deep, personal commitment to ensuring that individuals with disabilities, especially those with Down Syndrome, are not left behind in our society. In many communities, disability is still met with fear, isolation, and misinformation. Our work in Kahama and beyond is focused on one thing: dignity. We believe that every individual, regardless of their genetic makeup, has a right to health, education, and equal opportunity.

Our Mission

To improve the quality of life for individuals with Down Syndrome and other disabilities through targeted advocacy, community education, healthcare support, and direct intervention. We strive to empower families with the knowledge and resources they need to ensure their children can thrive and participate fully in all aspects of community life.

Our Vision

An inclusive society where individuals with disabilities are valued members of the community, respected for their unique contributions, and provided with equal opportunities to achieve their maximum potential without fear of discrimination or social stigma.

What is Down Syndrome?

Down syndrome is a naturally occurring chromosomal arrangement that has always been a part of the human condition. It is caused by the presence of an extra copy of chromosome 21 (also known as Trisomy 21). This extra genetic material alters the course of development and causes the physical and intellectual characteristics associated with Down syndrome.

It is crucial to understand that Down syndrome is not a disease, it is not contagious, and it is not the result of bad luck, sins, or curses. It is a biological variation that requires specialized medical attention, inclusive education, and most importantly, social acceptance.

Common Myths vs. Scientific Facts

Common Myths Scientific Facts
Down Syndrome is a contagious disease that can be spread. Fact: Down Syndrome is a genetic condition present from conception. It is absolutely impossible to "catch" it through contact or living together.
Individuals with Down Syndrome cannot learn, read, or be productive. Fact: With early intervention, proper therapy, and inclusive education, individuals with Down Syndrome can learn to read, write, and hold successful jobs.
It is caused by witchcraft, curses, or sins of the parents. Fact: It is a purely biological event caused by a chromosomal variation (Trisomy 21) during cell division at conception. It has no spiritual or superstitious cause.
People with Down Syndrome are always happy and childlike. Fact: Like everyone else, they are complex human beings who experience a full range of adult emotions, including happiness, sadness, anger, and disappointment.
They all look exactly the same. Fact: While they share certain physical traits, individuals with Down Syndrome look more like their own family members than they look like each other.

Advocacy & Programs

At FRAID Foundation, we don't just talk; we act. Our programs are designed to provide immediate relief while building long-term structures for inclusion in Tanzania.

1. Early Intervention & Healthcare Support

We provide families with essential guidance on managing Down Syndrome from birth. This includes coordinating medical check-ups (especially for heart and thyroid health), providing nutritional advice, and connecting parents with specialists who understand Trisomy 21.

2. Inclusive Education Initiative

We work directly with local schools and teachers in the Shinyanga region to promote inclusive classrooms. We advocate for children with disabilities to be taught alongside their peers, providing training on how to adapt teaching methods for diverse learners.

3. Family Support & Counseling

Having a child with a disability can be overwhelming. We host monthly support groups where parents can share experiences, reduce social isolation, and learn from one another in a safe, judgment-free environment.

4. Public Awareness & Stigma Reduction

Through community meetings, radio programs, and social media advocacy, we work to educate the public in Kahama about the reality of disabilities, replacing myths with scientific facts and human stories.

Donation Policy

FRAID Foundation is a registered non-profit organization. We are committed to 100% transparency and accountability in all our financial dealings. Every donation we receive is directly utilized for our core mission activities:

  • Medical Aid: Funding health screenings and specialized care for children with disabilities.
  • Education: Providing school materials and supporting inclusive education programs.
  • Advocacy: Organizing community workshops and awareness campaigns to end stigma.
  • Empowerment: Training parents and caregivers on home-based care and support techniques.

Reporting: We provide detailed annual impact reports to all our donors, showing exactly how their contributions have changed lives in Tanzania.
