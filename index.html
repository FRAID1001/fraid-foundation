What is Down Syndrome?

Down syndrome is a naturally occurring chromosomal arrangement that has always been a part of the human condition. It is caused by the presence of an extra copy of chromosome 21 (Trisomy 21). This extra genetic material alters the course of development and causes the characteristics associated with Down syndrome.

It is not a disease, it is not contagious, and it is not a result of bad luck or curses. It is a biological variation.

Common Myths vs. Scientific Facts

Common Myths Scientific Facts
Down Syndrome is a contagious disease. No. Down Syndrome is a genetic condition. It is impossible to "catch" it by touch or living with someone who has it.
People with Down Syndrome cannot learn. Fact: They have the potential to learn, attend school, and gain employment when provided with an inclusive environment and support.
It is caused by curses or witchcraft. This is a biological reality occurring at conception (Trisomy 21). It has no connection to spiritual curses or witchcraft.
People with Down Syndrome are always happy. They are human beings with a full range of emotions: they experience happiness, sadness, anger, and pain just like anyone else.

Advocacy & Programs

Rights & Inclusion: We advocate for the social inclusion and fundamental rights of individuals with Down Syndrome. Our goal is to dismantle stigma and ensure dignity for all.

1. Early Intervention & Healthcare Support

We provide families with essential guidance on managing Down Syndrome from birth, focusing on medical check-ups and nutritional support to improve long-term health outcomes.

2. Inclusive Education Initiative

Collaborating with local schools in Kahama to create inclusive learning environments, ensuring children with Down Syndrome have access to quality education.

3. Family Support & Counseling

Hosting monthly support groups for parents and caregivers to share experiences, reduce social isolation, and build a resilient community.
